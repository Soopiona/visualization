## Assignment 2: Good and Bad Data Visualization

### Requirements:

- Data visualizations are important tools for communication and convincing; we need to be able to evaluate the ways that data are presented in visual form to be critical consumers of information 
- To test your evaluation skills, locate two public data visualizations online, one good and one bad  
    - You can find data visualizations at https://public.tableau.com/app/discover or https://datavizproject.com/, or anywhere else you like! 
 For each visualization (good):  https://datavizproject.com/wp-content/uploads/leisure-time-men-women-chart-infographic.jpg
    - Explain (with reference to material covered up to date, along with readings and other scholarly sources, as needed) why you classified that visualization the way you did.
      ```
      The visualization clearly states its data source. And the contrast between the two sets of data, men and women, is very clear. The scale of each circle is written as well. On the title, "THAN WOMEN IN MOST COUNTRIES" is in bold and black highlighted. Through this text differentiation, the consumer can easily notice the author's intention.
      ```
    - How could this data visualization have been improved?  
      ```
       1. It would be better to group the characteristics of the data. (e.g., by continents,)
       2. I want to know why the scale is 50 minutes while the 1 hour is 60 minutes.
       3. If these countries are not the only countries in the given data, the author should mention that the data is not excluded or mention the full list somewhere else.
       4. Some countries have the same number, e.g., Poland(Men:301h, Women: 259h) and the Netherlands(Men:301h, Women:290h). How does the author rank between the same numbers? And why?
      ```


- For each visualization (bad):  https://datavizproject.com/wp-content/uploads/examples/Sk%C3%A6rmbillede-2017-02-23-kl.-16.36.42.png
    - Explain (with reference to material covered up to date, along with readings and other scholarly sources, as needed) why you classified that visualization the way you did.
      ```
     The image does not mention any source or reference. Because of this, the visualization has a trust issue. And the people in the visualization are targeting whether the global population or a certain country cannot be reached. Also, the year 2083 has no solid explanation for why the author chose it. The figure, 1in3, is too close to each other, so it gives an uncomfortable feeling to watch.
      ```
    - How could this data visualization have been improved?  
      ```
     1. It must include the source of the data.
     2. Reasoning the explanation of the chosen year, 2083, and the chosen age, age-60, as well.
     3. Figures need to be reorganized for better readability.
     4. The visualization is a bit blurred. If the author considers the consumer of this visualization in the online environment, the resolution must be increased.    
      ```
